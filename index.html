⚠️ Foods to Avoid

Raw or undercooked meat, fish, or eggs — risk of Listeria and Salmonella
High-mercury fish: swordfish, shark, king mackerel, tilefish
Unpasteurized dairy (soft cheeses like Brie, Camembert, blue cheese unless labeled pasteurized)
Raw sprouts (alfalfa, clover, radish) — bacteria risk
Deli meats and hot dogs unless heated until steaming
Alcohol — no safe amount is established during pregnancy
Caffeine limit: under 200mg/day (~1 small coffee)
Unwashed produce — always rinse fruits and vegetables thoroughly

Music, Voice & Sound
Your voice is the first sound they'll love. Here's how to use it.

🔬 What the Research Says

Weeks 1–18: Building the Ears

Baby's inner ear structures form by week 18, but sound processing develops through week 28. Until then, your voice carries through your body as vibration — incredibly intimate even before true hearing begins.

Weeks 18–28: Hearing Begins

From around week 18, babies begin to detect sound. By week 25, they can hear clearly. The most familiar sound? Your heartbeat, blood flow, and voice. Research shows newborns recognize and prefer their mother's voice immediately after birth.

Weeks 28–40: Active Listening

Baby reacts to sounds with movement. Music with rhythm can be calming. Studies show babies remember repeated melodies from the womb — playing a consistent lullaby now may help soothe them after birth.

🗣️ Talk to Your Baby

From about week 25 onward, your baby can hear and recognize your voice. Talking, singing, and reading aloud regularly helps build the neural pathways for language. Don't worry about feeling silly — try these prompts:

📖 Read aloudAny book works — even what you're reading yourself. The rhythm of language is what matters.
🎵 Sing a songPick one or two songs and repeat them daily. Your baby will recognize them after birth — it can be a powerful calming tool.
💬 Narrate your day"We're making dinner now... this is pasta, it smells delicious..." Exposure to vocabulary starts here.
❤️ Share your feelings"I can't wait to meet you. Your name is going to be..." Emotional tone communicates even before words are understood.

👂 What Baby Actually Hears in the Womb

The womb is not silent — it's a symphony! Baby hears a constant low rumble of your heartbeat (~72 bpm), blood rushing through the placenta (like white noise), the gurgles of digestion, your breathing, and most clearly of all, your voice resonating through your bones and chest. External sounds are muffled by about 30 decibels — like hearing music from the next room.
Healthy Habits & Wellness
How to sleep, move, rest, and thrive throughout pregnancy.

😴 Sleep Guidance

🛌
Left side sleeping (after 20 weeks)
Sleeping on your left side improves blood flow to baby and your kidneys. It's fine to switch sides — if you wake on your back, just roll over. A pregnancy pillow (between knees and behind back) helps enormously.
🌜
Consistent sleep schedule
Try to go to bed and wake at the same time each day. Fatigue is normal — naps are completely okay, especially in the first and third trimesters.
📵
Wind-down routine
Limit screens 30–60 min before bed. A warm (not hot) bath, light reading, or gentle stretching helps signal your body to rest.

🧘 Stress & Mental Wellness

🌬️
4-7-8 Breathing
Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale for 8. Activates the parasympathetic nervous system. Do 3–4 cycles when feeling anxious or overwhelmed.
🚶
Daily outdoor walks
Even 20 minutes outside reduces cortisol, improves mood, supports blood pressure, and gives you both fresh air and light. One of the simplest things you can do.
📓
Journaling
Writing down worries helps process them. Consider keeping a pregnancy journal — you'll cherish it later. Include what you're feeling, what you're hoping for, letters to baby.
💬
Talk about it
Anxiety during pregnancy is very common. Confide in your partner, a friend, or consider prenatal therapy. You don't need to "manage" everything alone.

🏃 Exercise During Pregnancy

Most women can and should exercise throughout a healthy pregnancy. Aim for ~150 min/week of moderate activity. Always check with your provider.

RECOMMENDED:

🚶 Walking 🏊 Swimming 🧘 Prenatal Yoga 🚴 Stationary Bike 💪 Light Strength Training 🩰 Low-Impact Aerobics 🤸 Pilates (modified)

AVOID / MODIFY:

Contact sports High altitude after T1 Lying flat on back after 20w Hot yoga / hot tubs Heavy lifting above head High-impact jumping
